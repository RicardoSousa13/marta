Marta Cabral

After 4 years studying Communication Design at FBAUP, Marta has found hidden between the covers of book a world she’s excited to dive in, interested to explore how to create captivating works in the field of editorial design.

For Marta, Design reflects her aim for perfection but a unexpected kind of perfection, far from the mathematical ideals achieved by computers and machines but a much more human version of it, a version of ancient almost mythological perfection, so imposing yet so human and full of little idiosyncrasies, often associated with her preferred techniques and practices like drawing, painting, printmaking, illustration and photography. Mixing and matching these practices she's able to create works that translate the almost lost art of authenticity honesty.

In this honesty Marta explores, in her original work, the reflection of her own memories (raging from special little details of her childhood to moments of a broken conversation she had just yesterday). Her work exposes her own experiences in such a way that those become universal to all of us.

BOOM da Década de 6O [2023]

Size
210mm × 297mm

Materials
Munken Pure 90gr
Canson Iris Vivaldi coloured paper 185gr

As a proposal, we were given a chapter from the book “Creation of the Tourism Fund and the Boom of the 1960s - Tourism as a Development Strategy” and asked to develop a magazine in black and white only, in A4 format in the “Swiss” style. The aim of this publication is to evoke the graphic minimalism of this era, stripping it of any visual futility.

🏃 Tá Fechado [2024]

With
Íris Silva
Mafalda Barros
Ricardo Sousa
Rita Soares
Rui Marques
Sofia Gouveia

Materials
Exhibition Leaflet
Entrance Wall
Poster
Social Media

This project was developed for the 2024 Finalists Exhibition for the Communication Design Degree at FBAUP. The exhibition celebrated the end of a cycle and its “archiving”. Having “the process” as the main theme we wanted to highlight a part of the work that is sometimes overlooked, but which is often the most important, as it reveals the true essence of the project. This year's distinguishing feature is the inclusion of the digital environment. These elements were chosen because this class was the first to enter the design world during the Covid-19 pandemic, and their first contact with university life was through a screen.

⌛ Lapse in Time [2022]

Size
210mm × 297mm

Materials
Fabriano Rosapina 220gr
Red cellophane 30gr
Bordeaux Velvet Sadipal Paper

Etymologically, nostalgia derives from the combination of nóstos (νόστος - “reunion”) and ἄλγος (álgos - “pain, suffering”), and can be considered the reencounter of suffering. In most interpersonal relationships, vulnerability arises late in life. This gives rise to a desire to witness the development of identities that are central to my existence. Just like the techniques used, human growth moves away from a simple straight line, being quite wandering, with a large interlude between these two instants.

♀ Virgem que pariu [2023]

Size
112mm × 223mm

With
Cláudia Barros
Mafalda Barros
Sofia Gouveia

Materials
Fluorescent Pink Clairefontaine Trophée coloured paper 80gr
Fuchsia Canson Iris Vivaldi coloured paper 185gr

Virgem que pariu seeks to invoke the Madonna Whore complex. An object of study for psychoanalysis, it explores the duality between erotic women (whores, sexually liberated) and chaste women (saints and sexually repressed). In this sense, this publication sets out to associate and oppose antagonistic concepts, deconstructing them. It is made up of paintings by men, fonts drawn by women and definitions from the priberam dictionary.

“Where such men love, they have no desire, and where they desire, they cannot love.” - Sigmund Freud

😱 Linguagem Artística [2022]

Size
145mm × 210mm

Materials
Fabriano Rosapina 220gr

Color refers to the light reflected from objects. Shape is the external appearance of objects. The line describes the shape, and when a line closes it gives rise to a shape. Composition means “to organize”, and is a concept that can be applied to any work of art. With this collection, the aim is to convey these three concepts through the smallest possible graphic elements, while maintaining a coherent line throughout the collection. The bas-relief elements add a focus for the reader's gaze, while also introducing a sensory component.

☁ Alfabelas [2022]

Size
300mm × 405mm

With
Mafalda Barros

Materials
Munken Pure 90gr
Ultramarine Canson Iris Vivaldi coloured paper 185gr

The aim of this publication is to compose a Roman alphabet using images of spaces at the Faculty of Fine Arts in Porto which, at first glance, bear no resemblance to the Roman alphabet, but which, with a certain framing and an attentive eye, metamorphose into these characters. In this way, an unusual and surprising alphabet is constructed. Analog photography proved to be the most appropriate medium for the project, giving it a certain timelessness. To give this work a greater purpose, we chose to associate it with the celebration of the institution's 250th anniversary.
